What is a slant route?

a slant route is a short, quick route where a receiver runs a few steps forward, then cuts sharply across the middle of the field at a 45-degree angle

What is a drag route?

similar to a slant route, a drag route is when a receiver takes a few steps downfield and then breaks towards the middle. It has a more "flat" trajectory from sideline to sideline than the angled route taken on a slant.

What is a flat route?

a basic, short route where a receiver, often a running back, tight end, or slot receiver, runs a few steps forward before quickly breaking outside towards the sideline. This route is typically used to create an early, short passing option or to draw defenders towards the sideline, creating space for other routes.

What is a comeback route?

a comeback route is a route where a receiver sprints downfield before turning back towards the sideline. It is typically only run by an outside receiver because in conjucnction with a back-shoudler throw it becomes very difficult for the defender to reach.

What is a curl route?

Also called a stop route, a curl route is when a receiver the receiver sprints directly downfield, then stop and curl back to the inside of the field, away from the closest sideline. Often used in conjunction with a comeback route

What is an out route?

An out route is similar to a flat route but run at much greater depth. The receiver runs 10-15 yards downfield and then breaks towards the sideline. Typically receivers catch this ball very clsoe to the sideline.

What is an in/dig route?

An in/dig route is a route where the receiver runs 10-15 yards straight downfield and then breaks towards the center of the field.

What is a corner route?

A corner route is a route where the receiver runs 10-15 yards straight downfield and then breaks outwards towards the deep outside corner of the field. This route can alleviate pressure from the middle of the field by drawing defenders towards the corner.

What is a post route?

A post route is when the receiver runs 10-15 yards downfield and then breaks in towards the middle of the field.

What is a go/fly route?

A go/fly route is when a receiver runs as fast and as straight as they can downfield. The goal is to trick a defender into thinking that they will break and create seperation with speed.

What is dagger concept?

a 3-man combo: a vertical route from the slot WR, a drag from the weak side, and a deep dig or square-in from the primary target

What is drive concept?

High low concept with a deep dig from the inside receiver and a shallow cross from the outside reciever

What is four verticals?

Passing concept where four receivers all go deep. Typically used in low time, high yardage situations where a big completion is neccesary.

What is mesh concept?

Simple crossing pattern two wide receivers crossing underneath at a mesh point. Usually tight end and slot receiver. If cover 2 change outside receiver to a post. Good against man and cover 4

What is mills concept?

The outside receiver runs a deep post and the inside runs a dig route. Great way to attack cover 4. Works against cover 1 and 3.

What is Defensive Pass Interference (DPI)?

When a defensive player significantly hinders an eligible offensive player's ability to catch a pass. Results in a spot foul and automatic first down.

In college football the maximum yardage gained from a DPI is 15 yards

What is a facemask penalty?

When a player intentionally grasps the facemask of an opponent's helmet, typically to pull push or twist. The punishment is a 15 yard penalty. This is strictly enforced as grabbing the facemask can be extremely dangerous.

What is a holding penalty?

When a player illegally grabs an opponent or their jersey to impede their movement. This is most commonly called on offensive linemen, but can be called on any player. The punishment is a 10 yard penalty.

What is a false start?

When an offensive player moves before the snap, typically the center or a wide receiver. The punishment is a 5 yard penalty.

What is a personal foul?

When a player commits an act of unnecessary roughness, such as hitting a defenseless player or using excessive force. The punishment is a 15 yard penalty.

What is a delay of game?

When the offense fails to snap the ball before the play clock expires. The punishment is a 5 yard penalty.

What is a roughing the passer?

When a defensive player makes illegal contact with the quarterback after they have thrown the ball. The punishment is a 15 yard penalty and an automatic first down.

What is taunting?

The NFL considers taunting to be any unsportsmanlike conduct directed at an opponent or their bench. This can include things like standing over opponents, moving toward their bench, or making gestures that are considered disrespectful or taunting in nature.

Who is a center?

Designated below with a "C", the center plays in the middle of the offensive line. They snap the ball to the quarterback to initiate the play. Typically standing between 6'2 and 6'4 and weighing between 280-340 lbs, centers tend to be quite large. They are also prized for their toughness.

Who is a quarterback?

Often considered the most important position on the team, a quarterback (QB) stands behind the center and receives the snap to initiate the play. Once the play has begun a quarterback typically hands it off to another player, passes it, or attempts to run with it themselves.

The QB is typically the only player in direct communication with the coach while the offense is on the field. It is his responsibility to relay instructions to the rest of the team.

The most important attributes for a QB are throwing ability and intelligence.

Who is a wide receiver?

A wide receiver, or WR, lines up closer to the sideline. Their role is primarily to catch passes downfield and gain yardage. A secondary function is to block on running plays

Wide receivers are typically around 6ft tall and 200 lbs. Their most important attributes are speed and catching ability.

Top wide receivers at the pro level sometimes have a reputation for dramatic behavior. This stems from their desire to be targeted more by the quarterback for passes.

Who is a running back?

A running back, or RB, lines up behind the quarterback and is responsible for running with the ball. They are also often used as a short passing option.

Running backs are typically around 5'10 and 220 lbs. Their most important attributes are speed and agility.

Who is a tight end?

A tight end, or TE, lines up next to the offensive line and can be used as a blocker or receiver. They are typically larger than wide receivers but smaller than offensive linemen.

Tight ends are typically around 6'4 and 250 lbs. Their most important attributes are catching ability and blocking ability.

Who is a tackle

A tackle plays on the edge of the offensive line. Their responsibility is to anchor the offensive line and block for the QB or RB

Since most quarterbacks are right handed, the left tackle is considered the premium position on the offensive line as they protect the QB's blindside

Tackles are typically around 6'5 and 300 lbs. Their most important attributes are strength and deceptive agility.

Who is a guard?

A guard plays on the inside of the offensive line. Their responsibility is to strengthen the offensive line and block for the QB or RB

Guards are typically around 6'3 and 300 lbs. Their most important attributes are strength and agility.
